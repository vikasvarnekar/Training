The DCO Process

An Express Document Change Order (DCO) is a quick Process for implementing Document and CAD Document changes within an organization. You can quickly release a new Document, or change, revise, or obsolete an existing document. Only certain identities can make modifications to a created Document under the supervision of a Team Manager. 

In this section, the term Document means an item of both Document and CAD Document item types.

Understanding the DCO Process

Product Team roles are engaged in the DCO workflow as follows:

  • The Team Manager is assigned to the Planning and Initial Review activities for reviewing Affected Documents and routing DCOs.

  • A Team Member is assigned to Draft Changes for making Changes to the Affected Documents.

In addition to the Product Team, the Express DOC Workflow includes the following roles:

  • A DCO Creator is a user who initially created and submitted the DCO in the system. The DCO Creator may or may not be the same person as other roles.

  • A DCO Change Coordinator (Owner) is the person responsible for managing the DCO from start to finish.

  • The Configuration Manager (CM) Group.

  • The Change Control Board (CCB) is responsible for the review and final approval of a DCO.
