Setting up Identities

Setting up Identities is similar to building a company organization chart. Use the following configuration process: 

  1. Create a Group Identity for each top-level structural unit (department). For example, a company may have the following departments: Engineering, Sales, Finances, and Marketing.

  2. Create identities for the top level department headings, for example: Engineering, Sales, and Marketing.

  3. For each top level heading, create a hierarchy of Group Identities reflecting the structure. For example, Engineering might have: Development, Support, and QA. Support, in turn, might further consist of: Customer Support, Documentation, and Training. Make sure you add the subordinate departments as members to their respective group identities.

  4. Create Teams for Express Change Management.

  5. Create Individual User Identities.

  6. Assign each of the User Identities to the appropriate Group Identities.

  7. Assign Identities involved in change management to Change Management Group Identities and Teams.

  8. Assign Identities responsible for managing Effectivity on Bill of Materials (BOM) to the Effectivity Management Identity.

  9. Once the departmental structure is in place, create individual user identities and add them to their respective departments. In Aras Innovator, one user identity can belong to more than one group.

  10. Configure access to ItemTypes in the Table of Contents (TOC).

Take a look at the Permissions matrix to see how these Identities are involved in the different lifecycle states and workflows of various items.
