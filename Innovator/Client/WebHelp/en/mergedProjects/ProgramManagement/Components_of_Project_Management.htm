Components of Project Management

The PMIProject Management Institute (Project Management Institute) suggests that Project Management includes the following areas of knowledge:

  1. Project Integration
  2. Scope Management
  3. Time Management
  4. Cost Management
  5. Quality Management
  6. Human Resource Management
  7. Communication Management
  8. Risk Management
  9. Procurement Management

Aras Program Management (PM) addresses the issues of Project Integration, Scope Management, Time Management, Resource Management and Communication Management. Other Innovator Solutions, such as Quality Planning, address Quality and Risk Management, while Product Engineering addresses Procurement and Cost Management.  The standard solutions can be extended to integrate Project Management with other business processes.

In Innovator, we provide a set of tools to assist organizations apply the following principles of Project Management:

  1. A project is a temporary endeavour to achieve a unique result.
  2. A project has 'progressive elaboration', which means that details may be decided or changed as the project proceeds
  3. The project manager takes personal responsibility to achieve the desired result
  4. The project manager's primary function is communication
  5. Work is broken down into smaller elements of work
  6. Responsibility for work elements is delegated to assignees
  7. Where an organization has several projects, consistency between projects is highly desirable
  8. Use of templates promotes consistency between similar projects
  9. Deliverables, the unique results, are the purpose of the project.

Our objectives are:

  1. A simple set of tools providing the core functionality
  2. All project information stored in a common database
  3. Communication of the current state of all projects on-line
  4. Communication to assignees of their assignments
  5. Delegation of responsibility for work elements to an Activity Leader
  6. Delegation for reporting work element completion to assignees
  7. Deliverables to be attached to projects
  8. Consistent status reporting for individual and portfolios of projects
  9. Extensibility of core functionality to meet organization specific needs
